History

According to folk etymology, the name Limbaži originated sometime in the 17th century. A recently arrived Swedish minister overheard the words "Limba" and "āži" (Latvian for 'male goats'). Mistakenly, he assumed this was the name of the place, and so the town was called "Limbaži". During the Middle Ages, as part of Livonia, Limbaži was a fortified town with stone walls, second in importance only to Riga.

Until the beginning of the 16th century, Lemsahl could be reached by seagoing vessels travelling up the Svētupe. Ships came from as far as Lübeck and Copenhagen to trade for honey, wax, lumber, grain, and furs. The small trading camp surrounding the castle grew into a large town, and was admitted to the Hanseatic League. Each year, Lemsahl hosted a conference attended by barons from all over Livonia, and the city hosted at least three other fairs throughout the year as well.

Munchhausen museum

The Munchausen Museum is a real catalyst in awakening the stories of the adventures of Colonel Hieronymus Carl Friedrich Baron von Munchausen. See weapons, hunting trophies and wax figures of Munchausen himself. Munchausen apparently spent the happiest years of his life with his wife Jacobina at the Dunte Manor in Vidzeme, Latvia, where people have centuries, incredible stories about the life of the baron continue to be told.

Limbaži medieval castle ruins and observation tower

The castle is one of the oldest defensive fortifications in Latvia. The castle's gate tower, where fragments of the unique portcullis have survived, offers a view of the reddish roofs of the Old Town of Limbaži. Several important details from the Gothic period have been preserved in the main gate. In the southern building, the Gothic window aisles with flat arches of pointed arches have been preserved. A spring flows from the northern slope of the mound. The surrounding landscape indicates that the castle is one of the oldest protective structures in Latvia.

Birini Castle Hotel

Birini Manor was built in 1860 on the top of a hill near the lake. It is a romantic site close to Riga, Sigulda and Saulkrasti beach. The Castle is surrounded by a picturesque park, lakes and manor buildings. It has a hotel, restaurant, splendid halls for seminars and special occasions. Recreation includes bathhouses, swimming beaches, walking routes in Emily’s Park and horseback riding.
